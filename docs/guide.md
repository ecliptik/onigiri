# Onigiri User Guide

Onigiri tracks calories, nutrients, sodium, and water on iPhone, iPad,
and Apple Watch. Everything you log is stored in **Apple Health** on
your device — no account, no servers, nothing to lose if you move on.

## Getting started

On first launch Onigiri walks you through:

1. **Health access** — Onigiri reads your energy burn, weight, and
   nutrition history, and writes the food, sodium, and water you log.
   Grant everything for the full experience; you can change it later in
   the Health app (Profile → Apps → Onigiri).
2. **A weight goal** (optional) — a target weight and date. You can set
   or change this any time on the Goal tab, or skip it entirely.
3. **Water settings** — your serving size and daily goal.

Until Apple Health has activity history, calorie budgets assume a
2,000 kcal/day burn; after a couple of weeks the app uses your real
14-day average.

## The Today screen

Today is home: your calorie balance for the day, at a glance.

- **The headline** is either your ± kcal balance (burned minus eaten) or
  a countdown of **kcal left** toward your daily budget — pick in
  Settings → Appearance. It turns amber when you're within a snack of
  the budget and orange when over.
- **Progress gauges** (Settings → Appearance) wrap the headline in a
  ring showing how much of the day's budget you've eaten.
- **Burned / Eaten** appear as cards below or flanking the headline
  (Settings → Appearance → Energy stats).
- The **tracked-metric row** shows two metrics of your choice — sodium
  and water by default; any nutrient with a custom target and icon via
  Settings → Tracked metrics.
- The **Daily goal card** shows deficit banked vs. target (or budget
  left, in maintain mode) with your reward badge filling as you go.
- **Details ›** under the headline opens the full nutrient breakdown
  for the day — macros, minerals, vitamins, and every logged entry.

**Browsing days:** swipe left/right anywhere, use the ‹ › arrows in the
top bar, or tap the day title to jump to any date from a month grid.
Logging while browsing a past day logs *to that day* — backfilling
yesterday's forgotten dinner takes two taps.

**The log** lists the day's entries grouped by meal (Breakfast, Lunch,
Dinner, Snack, Water). Tap a group to expand it, swipe an entry right
to edit, left to delete — deletes offer a one-tap **Undo**.

## Logging food

Tap the **+** button (bottom corner) to open the Log sheet.

- **Favorites / Foods / Meals** scopes list your library, with your
  favorites and recent items first. Tap an item, adjust the portion
  (quarter-step ± or type any quantity), and log.
- **Search** covers your library and, as you type, the online food
  database. Results show calories per serving; tapping one logs it and
  saves it to your library for next time.
- **Scan Barcode or Nutrition Label** — one camera, two tricks: point
  it at a barcode for a database lookup, or tap the shutter to
  photograph a nutrition-facts label and Onigiri reads the numbers off
  the label itself. Great for foods no database knows.
- **Describe it** (iPhones with Apple Intelligence) — type "two eggs
  and a slice of buttered toast" and the on-device model estimates the
  nutrition. Everything is editable before you save; nothing leaves
  your device.

**Online databases** (Settings → Online Database): barcode scans always
use [Open Food Facts](https://world.openfoodfacts.org). Text search
uses Open Food Facts, [USDA FoodData Central](https://fdc.nal.usda.gov),
or both — FDC needs a free api.data.gov key, stored in your device's
Keychain.

## Water

Water lives on Today: the hydration row shows progress toward your
daily goal, and one tap on it logs a serving. More ways to log a
serving:

- **Long-press the + button** (enable in Settings → Water)
- The **water widget** on your Lock Screen
- The **Control Center button**
- The **watch app's Log water button**

Serving size and daily goal are in Settings → Water.

## The Foods library

The Foods tab is your personal food database.

- **Favorites / Foods / Meals** scopes, searchable, sorted by ranked
  (favorites first, then recency), recent, or name.
- **Add a food** with the + button: type the numbers, scan a barcode or
  label, or search the online database from inside the form. Onigiri
  spots duplicates by barcode and offers to update the existing entry
  instead of creating a twin.
- **Star a food** to pin it to Favorites — on the phone and the watch.
- **Meals** bundle foods with quantities ("Chicken & rice" = 1 chicken
  breast + 2 rice bowls). Logging a meal logs every component in one
  tap. Meals can be favorites too.

Everything in the library syncs to the watch automatically.

## The Goal tab

Two modes:

- **Lose Weight** — set a target weight and date. Onigiri computes the
  **daily deficit** needed and your **calorie budget** (average burn
  minus deficit). The chart shows your weigh-ins (from a smart scale or
  manual Health entries), their 7-day average, and — once a downward
  trend exists — the date you'll hit your target at the current pace.
  A **Last 30 days** row compares what your logged deficits predicted
  against what the scale actually did.
- **Maintain** — eat what you burn. The budget is your expected daily
  burn; any deficit earns the day's badge.

Budgets are honest: they never assume less than what you've *actually*
burned today, so an active day's budget grows with you. Aggressive
plans (over 1,000 kcal/day deficit or under 1,500 kcal/day budget) get
flagged — the fix is a later target date, not eating less.

Weight comes from Apple Health when available; enter it manually
otherwise. Removing a goal never touches your logs.

## Calendar and streaks

The Calendar tab is the month at a glance:

- Days that met their goal earn your **reward badge** (the onigiri —
  or pick a trophy or any emoji in Settings → Appearance).
- The header counts badges this month and your **current streak**.
  Tap **Details** for month stats: days tracked, goal-met rate, total
  deficit, and the scale change over the month.
- Days are judged by the goal that was in force *on that day* — moving
  your goalposts today doesn't rewrite history.
- Days below a calorie threshold (Settings → Data → untracked
  threshold) count as untracked rather than failed — a forgotten
  logging day breaks the streak, but doesn't pretend you fasted.
- Tap any day for its full nutrition breakdown; tap **View day** to
  open it on Today for editing.

## Apple Watch

The watch app stands alone — it reads and writes the same Apple Health
data, and your food library syncs from the phone.

- **Home**: your balance (or kcal-left) headline, the day's gauge, and
  a one-tap **Log water** button.
- **Log**: favorites first, then recents — tap to log a food or meal.
  Tap an entry in the day list to adjust its calories with the crown or
  ± buttons, or remove it.
- **Metrics**: sodium, water, and your custom tracked metrics.
- **Complications**: calorie balance, water, streak, and a summary —
  for every watch face family, including corner and inline.

Logs sync between phone and watch through Apple Health itself —
typically within seconds in both directions.

## Widgets and complications

**Home Screen / Lock Screen (iPhone & iPad):**

- **Today** (small/medium/large) — the balance ring, burned/eaten,
  your tracked metrics, and a Log button that deep-links into the Log
  sheet.
- **Gauge** — the day's progress ring, small and glanceable.
- **Month Stats** — goal-met days and streak; tapping opens the
  Calendar.
- **Water** and **Streak** — lock-screen accessories.
- **Control Center** — a log-water button.

Widgets refresh within seconds of a log — including logs made on the
watch or in other Health apps.

## Reminders

Settings → Reminders, three independent nudges, all computed from your
actual day:

- **Meals** — a nudge if you haven't logged by mealtime.
- **Water pacing** — checks at 11 AM, 3 PM, and 7 PM, and only nudges
  while you're behind pace on your water goal.
- **Streak** — an 8 PM warning when today's goal isn't met yet and a
  streak is on the line.

Reminders that no longer apply (you logged; you caught up) simply don't
fire. Tapping one lands you in the right place to act on it.

## Settings reference

- **Appearance** — headline style (kcal balance / kcal left), progress
  gauges, energy stats layout (cards / beside balance), food & water
  icons, reward badge (onigiri, trophy, or any emoji).
- **Tracked metrics** — the two Today-row slots: sodium and water by
  default, or any nutrient with a goal or limit, custom target, and
  icon.
- **Water** — serving size, daily goal, hold-the-+-button logging.
- **Sodium** — daily limit (colors sodium across the app), shown or
  hidden.
- **Reminders** — see above.
- **Online Database** — text-search source: Open Food Facts, USDA
  FoodData Central (bring your own free key), or both.
- **Data** — export/import your library as JSON, automatic backups,
  untracked-day threshold.
- **Reset** — library, goals, settings, or everything. Apple Health
  data is never touched by any reset.

## Backups and your data

- **Logs** live in Apple Health — Onigiri's resets and even deleting
  the app never remove them. Health data is yours, exportable from the
  Health app itself.
- **The library** (foods, meals, goal, water settings) is backed up
  automatically to the app's Documents folder — visible in the Files
  app, five most recent kept. Settings → Data can export/import the
  same JSON anywhere, and imports are additive (nothing is overwritten
  or lost).

## iPad

On iPad, Onigiri uses the width: Today lays the summary beside the log,
and the tab bar can become a sidebar. All four orientations and every
multitasking mode (Split View, Slide Over, Stage Manager) are
supported.

## FAQ

**Why does my budget say 2,000 kcal?**
Apple Health doesn't have activity history yet (new device, or energy
sources not granted). The budget uses your real 14-day average once it
exists.

**Why can't I log?**
If Health write access is denied, every log fails — Today shows a hint
when this happens. Fix it in the Health app: Profile → Apps → Onigiri.

**A barcode isn't found.**
Open Food Facts is community-built; niche products may be missing.
Photograph the nutrition label instead — or add the food by hand and
scan the barcode into it, and it'll match forever after.

**Do I need an account?** No. There's nothing to sign up for, and no
way for anyone — including the developer — to see your data.

**Is this medical advice?** No. Budgets and projections are
informational estimates computed from your own numbers. Consult a
professional before making significant dietary changes.
